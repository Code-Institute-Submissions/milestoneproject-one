About

EXO is a South Korean – Chinese boy band created by Lee Soo Man, founder of SM Entertainment, in 2011. Originally formed of 8 Korean members and 4 Chinese members, the 12-member group showcased their official debut on April 8, 2012 with their first mini album Mama. Mama peaked the South Korean Gaon Musical Chart and placed 8th on the Billboard World Albums Chart. The group initially performed simultaneously as two sub units EXO-K (Korean Version) and EXO-M (Mandarin) in South Korea and China. The sensational break for the group came in 2013 with the title songs “Growl” and “Wolf” from their first repackaged album XOXO with record-breaking wins across South Korea, China, Japan and many other Asian countries along with creating a major foothold in the international music world and fandom. The years following the success and recognition post the album is affectionately mentioned as the “Growl-Wolf Era”. The group witnessed departure of three members during 2014-2015 due to injuries and personal issues.

EXO has again rewritten their records multiple times throughout the years by the continued huge success of their albums Exodus (2015), Ex’act (2016), The War (2017), Don’t Mess Up My Tempo (2018) and Obsession (2019). The title songs of each and every one of these albums has been massive hits on both local charts and global charts like the Oricon Albums Chart in Japan and the Billboard World Albums Chart. The “Power” track from the album The War became the first K-pop song to be performed at The Dubai Fountain at the Burj Khalifa lake in October 2018. In the same year EXO performed at the closing ceremony of the Winter Olympics held in Pyeongyang as the “nation’s pick”.

EXO has continued to attract music lovers of wide diversity in race, region and age to their fanbase by their unique and soulful music. It has even become for the fans to celebrate their Christmas with EXO’s winter albums. EXO continues to rule the world of music as the Kings of K-pop.
